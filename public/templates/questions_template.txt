Sample Question Format for PDF/DOCX Import
==========================================

Q1. What is the capital of France?
A) London
B) Paris
C) Berlin
D) Madrid
Answer: B
Marks: 2
Negative: 0.5
Time: 60
Difficulty: MEDIUM
Explanation: Paris is the capital and largest city of France, located on the Seine River.

Q2. What is 2 + 2?
A) 3
B) 4
C) 5
D) 6
Answer: B
Marks: 1
Negative: 0.25
Time: 30
Difficulty: EASY
Explanation: This is a basic addition problem. 2 + 2 = 4.

Q3. Who wrote "Romeo and Juliet"?
A) Charles Dickens
B) William Shakespeare
C) Jane Austen
D) Mark Twain
Answer: B
Marks: 2
Difficulty: MEDIUM
Explanation: William Shakespeare wrote the famous tragedy "Romeo and Juliet" around 1594-1596.

Q4. What is the chemical symbol for water?
A) H2O
B) CO2
C) O2
D) H2
Answer: A
Marks: 1
Negative: 0.33
Time: 45
Difficulty: EASY
Explanation: Water's chemical formula is H2O, meaning two hydrogen atoms bonded to one oxygen atom.

Q5. Which planet is known as the Red Planet?
A) Venus
B) Jupiter
C) Mars
D) Saturn
Answer: C
Marks: 1
Difficulty: EASY
Explanation: Mars is called the Red Planet due to iron oxide (rust) on its surface.

===========================================
FORMAT RULES:
===========================================

1. Start each question with Q followed by a number and a period (Q1., Q2., etc.)

2. Options can be formatted as:
   - A) Option text
   - A. Option text
   - (A) Option text
   - A: Option text

3. Answer line must include "Answer:" followed by the letter (A, B, C, or D)

4. Optional fields:
   - Marks: (default 1)
   - Negative: (negative marks, default 0)
   - Time: (time limit in seconds)
   - Difficulty: EASY, MEDIUM, or HARD (default MEDIUM)
   - Explanation: (shown after submission)

5. All optional fields are case-insensitive

6. Leave blank lines between questions for better readability

7. You can have 2-4 options per question (A-D)

===========================================
TIPS:
===========================================

- Keep question text clear and concise
- Ensure only one correct answer per question
- Use consistent formatting throughout
- Add explanations to help students learn
- Set appropriate time limits for complex questions
- Use difficulty levels for analytics
- Test with 2-3 questions before importing many
